FAR AWAY FROM HOME

Preparing For Work...

Did You Know?

In Singapore, the total overtime hours of 2/3 (68%) of foreign construction workers, exceeds the legal maximum of 72 overtime hours per month.

Of these, at least 1/3 (23%) worked twelve and a half hours or more in a single day — which also violates the legal maximum of 12 hours, daily.

Sadly, they do not have much say over the hours that they have to work overtime as their project schedule is already fixed by their supervisors.

During Work...

Did You Know?

In Singapore, around 200,000 of foreign workers in Singapore - Chinese migrants, work long hours for low pay in frequently hazardous conditions and are often abused by employers and labor contractors.

During Lunch Break...

Did You Know?

In Singapore, the food situation for most migrant workers is, undesirable. Many get their meals from cheap catering services that prepare thousands of meals in advance.

This results in the food becoming rancid by the time it is consumed. In addition, most workers do not return from work, until 7 or 8 pm.

When they do return to their dorms, often, there are not enough cooking facilities available. One example of a meal would be — a piece of fish in curry that has sat “the morning heat for half a day”, a portion of “watery” dal and nearly a kilo of cold rice.
